Simulation Scenarios

Practice using the expressions in realistic meeting contexts. Read the scenario and then use the provided phrases to form your response.

Scenario 1: Strategic Planning Meeting

Your team is developing a new strategy. You need to turn the high-level plan into concrete actions, and you want to ensure the team is inspired and focused on the same goal.

  • To put the plan into practice: "Our next step is to **operationalize the strategy** by defining key performance indicators."
  • To inspire the team: "We must **galvanize the team** behind this new initiative to ensure its success."

Scenario 2: Project Management Update

During a project update, a team member is getting bogged down in minor details. You need to pull the conversation back to the main objective. You also need to confirm that a decision has been made to phase out an older system.

  • To get back to the point: "Let's not get **in the weeds** on this; what is our primary objective for this quarter?"
  • To announce a project's end: "I've been told we will **sunset** the legacy system by the end of the year."

Scenario 3: Market Analysis Discussion

You are in a meeting to discuss recent market data. You want to see if a new product idea will be successful, and you need to get more detailed information on a specific market segment. You also want to show that you are capable of handling multiple roles.

  • To test an idea: "Let's **run it up the flagpole** and see if the marketing team thinks this new product will **gain traction** with our target audience."
  • To ask for more detail: "We need to **do a deep dive** into the data from the Southeast region."
  • To express capability: "I have the **bandwidth** to take on this research task myself."
